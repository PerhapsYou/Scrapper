SLU VISION-MISSION:  
Saint Louis University is envisioned as an excellent missionary and transformative  
educational institution zealous in developing human resources imbued with the  
Christian Spirit and who are creative, competent, and socially involved.  

STELA VISION-MISSION:  
The School of Teacher Education and Liberal Arts (STELA) is envisioned as an institution  
for excellence in Teacher and Liberal Arts Education committed to the CICM mission of  
“missio et excellentia” in the formation and training of competent, creative,  
socially-involved professionals imbued with the Christian Spirit.  

==========================================================================  

PROGRAM EDUCATIONAL OBJECTIVES (PEO)  
Three to five years after graduation, graduates of the Bachelor of Secondary Education  
are expected to:

- Serve as in-service teachers imbued with a Christian philosophy of education,  
  cognizant of the dignity of all learners.  
- Demonstrate relevant and responsive knowledge and skills that foster creativity,  
  critical thinking, and scholarly research needed for national and international  
  development.  
- Exhibit excellence in technological, pedagogical, and content knowledge (TPACK) in  
  facilitating the teaching-learning process of their respective courses.  
- Willingly share their expertise as professional teachers to respond to the needs of the  
  local, regional, national, and international communities.  

==========================================================================  

PROGRAM LEARNING OUTCOMES (PLO)  
Graduates of the Bachelor of Secondary Education program are expected to:

1. Apply the latest developments in the specific field of practice.  
2. Participate in the generation, dissemination, and utilization of new knowledge and  
   developmental projects.  
3. Communicate effectively orally and in writing using both English and Filipino.  
4. Work effectively and independently in multi-disciplinary and multi-cultural teams.  
5. Act in accordance with professional, social, and ethical responsibility.  
6. Promote Filipino historical and cultural heritage.  
7. Demonstrate high-level literacy, communication, numeracy, critical thinking, and  
   learning skills.  
8. Facilitate learning at the secondary education level guided by principles and methods  
   of facilitating learning.  
9. Integrate meaningfully and comprehensively the knowledge of the subject matter into  
   real-life situations of learners.  
10. Apply a wide range of teaching and facilitating process skills including curriculum  
    development, lesson planning, materials development, educational assessment,  
    and teaching approaches.  
11. Innovate on effective alternative teaching and learning approaches, methodologies,  
    and strategies to improve student learning.  
12. Facilitate meaningful learning of diverse types of learners, in diverse types of learning  
    environments, using a wide range of teaching knowledge and skills.  
13. Collaborate with colleagues and other experts in their own fields for continuous  
    professional development and a better fulfillment of their mission as teachers.  
14. Create an inclusive, learning-friendly environment that promotes the potential of all  
    learners.  
15. Practice Christian values in their personal and professional endeavors as Louisians in  
    the service of the CICM mission.
