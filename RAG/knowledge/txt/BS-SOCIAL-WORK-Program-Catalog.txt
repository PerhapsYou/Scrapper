SLU Vision-Mission:  
Saint Louis University is envisioned as an excellent missionary and transformative educational institution zealous in developing human resources who are imbued with Christian Spirit and who are creative, competent, and socially involved.

School Vision-Mission (STELA):  
The School of Teacher Education and Liberal Arts (STELA) is envisioned as an institution for excellence in Teacher and Liberal Arts Education, committed to the CICM mission of “missio et excellentia” in forming competent, creative, socially-involved professionals imbued with Christian Spirit.

Program Educational Objectives (PEO):  
Three to five years after graduation, BS Social Work graduates are expected to:  
1. Model the social work profession through acceptance of human diversity, inclusion, and social equity with Christian values, moral character, honesty, and professional integrity.  
2. Pursue advanced social work education, research, and development for the promotion of human rights, social justice, and global solidarity.  
3. Demonstrate competence in addressing social issues using social work frameworks (casework, group work, community organizing) and professional judgment.  
4. Advocate for the rights of the vulnerable and marginalized through relationship-building, resource mobilization, and delivery of effective social services.

Program Learning Outcomes (PLO):  
Graduates of the BS Social Work program are expected to:  
- Evaluate global and national issues affecting quality of life (e.g., discrimination, oppression).  
- Assess the relevance and accessibility of social welfare policies, programs, and services.  
- Demonstrate critical self-reflection for personal and professional growth.  
- Apply updated social work knowledge and skills in generalist practice (therapeutic, protective, preventive, transformative).  
- Use knowledge of human behavior in the person-in-situation context for assessment and intervention.  
- Compile a professional portfolio including recordings, case studies, and social work documentation.  
- Engage in advocacy to promote socio-economic and cultural rights.  
- Generate resources and build partnerships.  
- Practice inclusivity and promote diversity in client systems.  
- Communicate effectively in English and Filipino, both orally and in writing.  
- Uphold social work values and ethical principles.  
- Work independently and collaboratively in multidisciplinary and multicultural teams.  
- Promote Filipino cultural heritage.  
- Practice Christian values in personal and professional life in line with the CICM mission.

Curriculum:
