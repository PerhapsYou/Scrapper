== SCHOOL HISTORY ==
The academic year 1954–1955 marked the birth of the Saint Louis University School of Law (then called Saint Louis College of Law). It was authorized under Temporary Permit No. 365, s. 1954, issued by Secretary of Education P.M. Endencia and recommended by Daniel Salcedo, Assistant Director of Private Schools.

Timeline of Course Offerings:
- 1954: First-Year Law course
- 1955: Second-Year Law course
- 1956: First to Third Year Law courses
- 1957: Fourth-Year Law course offered; government recognition granted for First to Third Year
- 1958: Government recognition for Fourth-Year Course

Notable Faculty during Early Years:
- Dean Francisco Reyes  
- Judge Federico Cabato  
- Atty. Fernando Romero  
- Atty. Conrado Sabelino  
- Atty. Bernardo Ronquillo  
- Judge Jesus De Vera  
- Chief Justice Manuel V. Moran  
- Chief Justice Roberto Concepcion

In Academic Year 2010–2011, the name was changed from SLU College of Law to SLU School of Law.

== VISION & MISSION ==
The SLU School of Law is committed to providing excellent legal education toward the holistic formation of Christian, ethical, and competent professionals. These professionals are envisioned to serve the community and actively contribute to nation-building.

== GOALS AND OBJECTIVES ==
- To produce ethical, competent, and principled lawyers.  
- To maintain high standards of quality in legal education through adequate facilities and learning resources.  
- To provide free legal services to indigent members of the Baguio-Benguet community via the Chief Justice Manuel V. Moran Community Legal Assistance.  
- To foster students’ creativity through co- and extra-curricular activities.
