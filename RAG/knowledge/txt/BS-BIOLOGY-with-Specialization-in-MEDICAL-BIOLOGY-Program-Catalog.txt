SLU VISION-MISSION  
Saint Louis University is envisioned as an excellent missionary and  
transformative educational institution zealous in developing locally responsive,  
globally competitive, and empowered human resources who are creative,  
competent, socially involved, and imbued with Christian spirit.  

SCHOOL VISION-MISSION  
The School of Nursing, Allied Health and Biological Sciences envisions herself  
as the seat of excellence in Nursing, Allied Health and Biological Sciences  
Education that is dynamic and inspired by a Christian way of living, producing  
globally competitive and value-laden nurses and health-care professionals in  
service of humanity.

PROGRAM EDUCATIONAL OBJECTIVES (PEO)  
Three to five years after graduation, graduates of BS Biology with Specialization  
in Medical Biology are expected to:

1. Demonstrate in-depth knowledge of fundamental biological concepts and  
   skills needed in research, teaching, biology-based industries, and further  
   studies (e.g., medicine, graduate programs).  
2. Apply basic mathematical and statistical computations and use appropriate  
   technologies in the analysis of biological data.  
3. Use the latest research developments in the field to contribute to  
   sustainable development goals (SDG 3: Health, SDG 4: Quality Education,  
   SDG 13: Climate Action).  
4. Effectively communicate biological knowledge in oral and written forms  
   using English and Filipino.  
5. Work effectively and independently in multi-disciplinary and multicultural  
   teams.  
6. Promote Filipino historical and cultural heritage through biological research  
   and applications (SDG 11: Sustainable Cities and Communities).  
7. Exhibit professional, social, and ethical responsibility, especially in practicing  
   intellectual property rights.  
8. Participate in the conservation and sustainability of natural resources.  
9. Practice Christian values in personal and professional endeavors as Louisians  
   in the service of the CICM mission.  

================================================================================  
PROGRAM LEARNING OUTCOMES (PLO)  
Graduates of the BS Biology with specialization in Medical Biology are expected to:

1. Uphold the dignity of all life forms exemplifying Christian values in the  
   practice of their profession.  
2. Apply research methods and techniques using native resources to address  
   environmental, health, and socio-economic issues.  
3. Exhibit the fundamental knowledge and skills for biology-related careers,  
   postgraduate studies, and medical school.  
4. Participate actively in community activities with commitment, dedication,  
   and concern for others.

ADMISSION POLICIES  

FRESHMAN STUDENTS  
- Must pass the SLU College Entrance Examination (SLU-CEE).  
- SLU-CEE is conducted from mid-October to mid-December.  
- Admission starts mid-April.

TRANSFER STUDENTS  
- Must undergo a Qualifying Examination (QE).  
- If qualified, will proceed to Personality Test and Interview.  
- Foreign transferees must also pass the English Proficiency Test (EPT).

FOREIGN STUDENTS  
- Must apply at least 6 months before the start of the term.  
- Must be in Baguio City 4 weeks before classes begin.  
- Required to take the EPT, SLU-CEE/QE, and Personality Test.  
- Must possess English proficiency and a valid Student Visa.  
- Undergo pre-admission processing via Student Affairs Office.
