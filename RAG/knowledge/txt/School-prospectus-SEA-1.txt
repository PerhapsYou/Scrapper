VISION & MISSION  
-----------------------------------------------------------  
The School of Engineering and Architecture envisions itself as an exemplary educational institution for engineering and architecture.  
It is dedicated to elevating instruction, research, and extension to a higher level of competence and creativity, committed to shaping  
the socio-technical environment founded on Christian values.

-----------------------------------------------------------  
HISTORY  
-----------------------------------------------------------  
- Began in 1952 with Basic Engineering subjects taught above a silversmith shop (now SLU Elementary Department).  
- Started with only two regular instructors, with support from faculty in Education, Commerce, and Liberal Arts.  
- The department was supervised by Rev. Dr. Gerard Decaestecker, then Rector of Saint Louis College.  

-----------------------------------------------------------  
GOALS AND OBJECTIVES  
-----------------------------------------------------------  
1. Enhance intellect and creativity through excellence in instruction, research, and extension work.  
2. Promote the highest ethical standards among scholars/professionals across disciplines.
