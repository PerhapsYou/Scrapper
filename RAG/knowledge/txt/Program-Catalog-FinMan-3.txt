PROGRAM EDUCATIONAL OBJECTIVES (PEO)  
Three to five years after graduation, graduates of the BSBA Financial Management Program are expected to:  
1. Apply Christian values and ethical behavior in their personal and professional endeavors in a globalized, multicultural, and technology-driven world.  
2. Develop creative solutions through research and innovative projects to address discipline-related problems and issues.  
3. Demonstrate the finance profession and pursue life-long learning in their personal and professional development.  
4. Engage in community-based programs to promote sustainable solutions that will address global, political, societal, and environmental concerns.

PROGRAM LEARNING OUTCOMES (PLO)  
Graduates of the program are expected to:  
1. Communicate business concepts and strategies effectively, orally and in writing, in both English and Filipino, using appropriate technology.  
2. Apply financial principles, theories, and concepts in public and private entities.  
3. Evaluate socio-political and economic factors in multi-disciplinary and multicultural local and global business environments.  
4. Exhibit relevant knowledge in research in the field of financial management.  
5. Participate in community-based programs to enhance financial literacy and fund management capabilities of individuals.  
6. Demonstrate ethical behavior in handling money, financial instruments, and all financial dealings.  
7. Promote Filipino historical and cultural heritage through patriotism and active participation in improving the Philippine financial system.  
8. Practice Christian values in their personal and professional lives in service of the CICM mission.

ADMISSION POLICIES  

**Undergraduate Freshman Students**  
- Must pass the SLU College Entrance Examination (SLU-CEE).  
- SLU-CEE is held from mid-October to mid-December.  
- First semester admission begins mid-April.

**Transfer Students**  
- Permitted in all programs except BMLS.  
- Must pass a Qualifying Examination (QE), then take a Personality Test and Interview.  
- Foreign transferees must also pass the English Proficiency Test (EPT).

**Graduate Students**  
- Master’s: Must have a baccalaureate degree from a recognized institution.  
- Doctorate: Must hold a master’s degree in a related field from a recognized institution.

**Foreign Students**  
- Must apply at least 6 months before the academic term.  
- Must arrive in Baguio at least 4 weeks before classes start.  
- Required to take EPT, SLU-CEE/QE/GPEE, and Personality Test.  
- Undergo preadmission processing at the Student Affairs Office.  
- Must secure a valid Student Visa through the Registrar’s Office.
